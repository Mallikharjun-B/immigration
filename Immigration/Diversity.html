Eligibility

The Diversity Visa Program, also known as the DV lottery, provides 55,000 visas annually to individuals from countries with low immigration rates to the United States. To be eligible, applicants must:

  • Be a native of a qualifying country.
  • Have at least a high school education or its equivalent, or two years of work experience within the past five years in an occupation that requires at least two years of training or experience.

Each year, the U.S. Department of State publishes a list of eligible countries and detailed requirements for the Diversity Visa Program.

Application Process

The application process for the Diversity Visa Program consists of the following steps:

Step 1: Submit an Entry

Applicants must submit their entries online through the official Diversity Visa Program website during the designated registration period. Only one entry per person is allowed.

Step 2: Selection

Entries are selected randomly through a computerized lottery system. Selected applicants will be notified through the Entrant Status Check on the Diversity Visa Program website.

Step 3: Complete the DS-260 Form

Selected applicants must complete the DS-260 Immigrant Visa and Alien Registration Application form online and submit the required documentation.

Step 4: Attend the Interview

Applicants must attend an interview at the U.S. Embassy or Consulate in their home country. They must bring their confirmation page, passport, photographs, and other required documents.

Step 5: Receive the Visa

If approved, applicants will receive their Diversity Visa and can travel to the United States to become lawful permanent residents.
