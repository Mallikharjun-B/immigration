Highly Skilled Workers

Employment-based immigration for highly skilled workers includes individuals with extraordinary abilities in the sciences, arts, education, business, or athletics, outstanding professors and researchers, and multinational executives and managers. These individuals may be eligible for the EB-1 visa category.

Requirements

To qualify, applicants must demonstrate sustained national or international acclaim and recognition in their respective fields through extensive documentation and evidence.

Professionals

Employment-based immigration for professionals encompasses those with advanced degrees or exceptional abilities in the sciences, arts, or business. These individuals may be eligible for the EB-2 visa category.

Requirements

Applicants must have an advanced degree or its equivalent, or demonstrate exceptional ability in their professional field. This includes substantial documentation and recognition of their expertise.

Other Workers

Employment-based immigration for other workers includes skilled workers, professionals, and unskilled workers who may qualify for the EB-3 visa category. This category covers a broad range of employment opportunities.

Requirements

Skilled workers must have at least two years of job experience or training. Professionals must possess a U.S. bachelor's degree or its foreign equivalent, and unskilled workers must be capable of performing unskilled labor, not of a temporary or seasonal nature, for which qualified workers are not available in the United States.

Investors

The EB-5 visa category is for immigrant investors who are willing to invest significant capital in a new commercial enterprise in the United States and create jobs for U.S. workers.

Requirements

To qualify, investors must invest $1 million in a new commercial enterprise, or $500,000 in a targeted employment area (high-unemployment or rural area), and create or preserve at least 10 full-time jobs for qualifying U.S. workers within two years of the immigrant investor's admission to the United States.
