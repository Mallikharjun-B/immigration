Family-Based Immigration

Family-based immigration allows U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents (LPRs) to bring certain family members to the United States. This category includes visas for immediate relatives and family preference categories.

Immediate Relatives

Immediate relative visas are available to spouses, unmarried children under the age of 21, and parents of U.S. citizens. These visas are not subject to numerical limits, meaning there are no caps on the number of visas issued each year.

Family Preference

Family preference visas are available to more distant relatives of U.S. citizens and certain relatives of LPRs. These categories include:

  • First Preference (F1): Unmarried sons and daughters of U.S. citizens, and their minor children, if any.
  • Second Preference (F2A): Spouses and children of LPRs.
  • Second Preference (F2B): Unmarried sons and daughters (21 years of age and older) of LPRs.
  • Third Preference (F3): Married sons and daughters of U.S. citizens, and their spouses and minor children.
  • Fourth Preference (F4): Brothers and sisters of adult U.S. citizens, and their spouses and minor children.

Unlike immediate relative visas, family preference visas are subject to annual numerical limits, leading to potential waiting periods for visa availability.
