Refugees

Refugees are individuals who have fled their home countries due to persecution, war, or violence. They seek protection and resettlement in a host country like the United States. Here are key points about refugees:

  • Refugees must demonstrate a well-founded fear of persecution based on race, religion, nationality, political opinion, or membership in a particular social group.
  • They go through a rigorous screening process by the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) and the U.S. government before being approved for resettlement.
  • Once approved, refugees are eligible for various benefits and services to help them integrate into their new communities.

The U.S. Refugee Admissions Program (USRAP) oversees the admission and resettlement of refugees in the United States.

Asylees

Asylees are individuals who are already present in the United States or at a port of entry and seek protection because they have suffered persecution or have a well-founded fear of persecution in their home country.

Key information about asylees includes:

  • Asylees must apply for asylum within one year of their arrival in the United States, unless there are exceptional circumstances.
  • They undergo an interview with an asylum officer to determine if they meet the criteria for asylum.
  • If granted asylum, they can live and work in the United States and apply for permanent residency (green card) after one year.

The U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) handles asylum applications and adjudications.
