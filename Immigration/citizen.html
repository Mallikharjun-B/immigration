Eligibility Requirements for Citizenship

Age and Residency

To apply for citizenship, applicants must be at least 18 years old and have been a lawful permanent resident (green card holder) for at least 5 years.

Continuous Residence

Applicants must have resided continuously in the United States for at least 5 years prior to applying for citizenship.

Good Moral Character

Applicants must demonstrate good moral character, including adhering to U.S. laws, paying taxes, and not committing certain crimes.

Language and Civics Test

Applicants must pass an English language proficiency test and a civics test covering U.S. history and government.

Naturalization Test

English Proficiency

The English language test assesses an applicant's ability to speak, read, and write in English.

Civics Test

The civics test covers important U.S. history and government topics. Applicants must correctly answer a set number of questions.

Exemptions and Accommodations

Some applicants may be exempt from the English language test based on age and length of residency in the U.S. Accommodations are available for individuals with disabilities.

Preparing for the Test

Study materials, including official guides and practice tests, are available to help applicants prepare for the naturalization test.

Application Process for Citizenship

Form N-400

Applicants must complete Form N-400, Application for Naturalization, and submit it along with required documents and fees.

Biometrics Appointment

Applicants will be scheduled for a biometrics appointment to provide fingerprints, photographs, and a signature.

Interview

Applicants must attend an interview with USCIS where they will be tested on English proficiency and civics knowledge.

Decision

USCIS will notify applicants of their decision regarding citizenship. If approved, applicants attend an oath ceremony to complete the process.

Citizenship Through Birth

Birth in the United States

Individuals born in the United States automatically acquire U.S. citizenship, regardless of the citizenship status of their parents.

Birth to U.S. Citizen Parents

Individuals born outside the United States to at least one U.S. citizen parent may be eligible for U.S. citizenship at birth.

Derivation and Acquisition

Derivation and acquisition are processes through which individuals may acquire U.S. citizenship through their parents, based on specific circumstances and requirements.

Derivation and Acquisition

Derivation of Citizenship

Derivation of citizenship refers to acquiring U.S. citizenship through a parent who is a U.S. citizen, typically when certain conditions are met before the age of 18.

Acquisition of Citizenship

Acquisition of citizenship refers to becoming a U.S. citizen after birth through the naturalization process for individuals born outside the United States to at least one U.S. citizen parent.

Retention Requirements

Individuals who acquire U.S. citizenship through derivation or acquisition must meet certain retention requirements to maintain their citizenship status.
