Temporary Protected Status (TPS)

Temporary Protected Status (TPS) is granted to individuals from certain countries affected by armed conflict, natural disasters, or other extraordinary conditions that make it unsafe for their nationals to return.

Key points about TPS include:

  • TPS allows eligible individuals to live and work in the United States for a limited period without fear of deportation.
  • It does not lead to permanent residency, but beneficiaries can apply for other immigration benefits if eligible.
  • TPS is granted by the U.S. government for specific countries and may be extended or terminated based on conditions in the home country.

Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)

DACA provides temporary protection from deportation and work authorization for undocumented immigrants who came to the United States as children and meet certain eligibility criteria.

Key points about DACA include:

  • Applicants must have arrived in the United States before the age of 16 and continuously resided since June 15, 2007.
  • DACA recipients (known as Dreamers) can renew their status every two years, subject to meeting ongoing requirements.
  • Although DACA does not provide a path to permanent residency, it allows recipients to work legally and obtain a Social Security number.
